Zodiac Constellations

Explore the 12 zodiac constellations that have guided humanity for millennia. Each sign has unique characteristics and astronomical significance dating back to ancient times.

Aries

March 21 - April 19

Aries, the Ram, is a mid-sized constellation representing the golden ram of Greek mythology. Its brightest star is Hamal, an orange giant about 66 light-years from Earth.

Taurus

April 20 - May 20

Taurus, the Bull, features the bright red giant Aldebaran and the Pleiades star cluster. In mythology, it represents Zeus in bull form.

Gemini

May 21 - June 20

Gemini, the Twins, represents Castor and Pollux from Greek mythology. Its two brightest stars are named after these twin brothers.

Cancer

June 21 - July 22

Cancer, the Crab, is the faintest of the zodiac constellations. It contains the Beehive Cluster, a beautiful open star cluster visible to the naked eye.

Leo

July 23 - August 22

Leo, the Lion, is one of the most recognizable constellations. Its brightest star, Regulus, was considered one of the four "royal stars" by ancient Persians.

Virgo

August 23 - September 22

Virgo, the Maiden, is the second-largest constellation. It contains the bright star Spica and the Virgo Cluster of galaxies.

Libra

September 23 - October 22

Libra, the Scales, is the only zodiac constellation representing an inanimate object. It was once considered part of Scorpius's claws.

Scorpius

October 23 - November 21

Scorpius, the Scorpion, is one of the brightest constellations. Its red supergiant star Antares rivals Mars in color and brightness.

Sagittarius

November 22 - December 21

Sagittarius, the Archer, points toward the center of our galaxy. It's rich in star clusters and nebulae, including the Lagoon and Trifid Nebulae.

Capricornus

December 22 - January 19

Capricornus, the Sea-Goat, is one of the oldest constellations. In Babylonian astronomy, it represented the god Ea, who had the upper body of a goat and lower body of a fish.

Aquarius

January 20 - February 18

Aquarius, the Water Bearer, is one of the oldest recognized constellations. It lies in a region often called the "Sea" due to its many water-related constellations.

Pisces

February 19 - March 20

Pisces, the Fish, represents two fish tied together. The constellation contains the Vernal Equinox point, where the sun crosses the celestial equator each spring.
